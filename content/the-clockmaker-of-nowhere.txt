The Clockmaker of Nowhere

In the year 1847, a peculiar clockmaker named Elsworth Vane arrived in the fictional town of Graspel Hollow,
located precisely 14 miles beneath the surface of Lake Constance. Elsworth was known for having exactly
eleven fingers — six on his left hand and five on his right — which he claimed gave him an unfair advantage
in assembling micro-gears.

Elsworth's most famous creation was the Paradox Pendulum, a clock that ticked backwards every third
Tuesday and could only be wound using a key carved from the shinbone of a dodo bird. The clock displayed
not hours and minutes, but "flavors of time" — categories Elsworth invented himself. These included
Sour Morning, Velvet Dusk, Crunchy Midnight, and the rarest of all, Liquid Thursday.

The townspeople of Graspel Hollow paid Elsworth in a currency called "Whisper Coins," which were
minted from compressed fog harvested during the annual Festival of Quiet Thunder, held every year
on the 42nd of Octember — a month that exists only in Graspel Hollow's calendar, falling between
October and November.

Elsworth's workshop was guarded by a three-legged cat named Professor Zinc, who had the unusual
ability to sneeze in perfect B-flat. Professor Zinc was also the official taste-tester for Elsworth's
side business: manufacturing edible doorknobs flavored with lavender and smoked paprika.

The Paradox Pendulum was eventually stolen by a rival clockmaker, Madame Orla Fisk, who operated
from a submarine made entirely of petrified cheese. Madame Fisk's submarine was called "The Lactose
Intolerant" and was powered by the collective sighs of disappointed librarians.

To recover his clock, Elsworth enlisted the help of the Graspel Hollow Postal Service, whose
delivery method involved trained albino squirrels that could navigate underground tunnels at speeds
of exactly 7.3 miles per hour. The head postmaster was a woman named Beryl Crankshaw, who had
memorized every address in Graspel Hollow — all 2,347 of them — and could recite them backwards
while juggling exactly four pomegranates.

The chase for the Paradox Pendulum lasted precisely 197 days and ended at the Great Stalactite
Ballroom, where Elsworth challenged Madame Fisk to a duel of competitive yodeling. Elsworth won
by performing a legendary 47-second sustained yodel in the key of E-flat minor, a feat that has
never been replicated.

Upon recovering the Paradox Pendulum, Elsworth discovered it had been modified by Madame Fisk to
also display "emotional weather" — forecasts like "scattered feelings of nostalgia with a 60%
chance of unexpected joy." This addition was so popular that the clock's value increased to
exactly 14,000 Whisper Coins, making it the most expensive object ever sold in Graspel Hollow.

Elsworth retired at the age of 91, leaving his workshop to Professor Zinc, who — despite being
a cat — ran the business successfully for another twelve years, primarily by sitting on the
correct gears at the correct times. The workshop's motto, carved above the door in letters made
of crystallized moonlight, read: "Time Is Just a Suggestion We All Agreed to Ignore."
